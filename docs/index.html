HD Hyundai Signs Deal for Humanoid Welding Robots in Shipyards

HD Hyundai said Monday it has signed a joint development agreement with HD Korea Shipbuilding & Offshore Engineering, HD Hyundai Robotics, and US robotics firm Persona AI to develop and commercialize welding humanoid robots for shipyards. Persona AI will develop a bipedal humanoid platform designed for stable movement in complex shipyard environments. The partners aim to develop humanoid robots performing high-skill tasks such as welding, mobility, perception, and precision control, with a key focus on replicating the workflows of experienced welders using physical AI. The initiative will deliver prototype humanoids by the end of 2026, with field testing and full commercial deployment scheduled to begin in 2027.

Why it matters → Shipbuilding is among the harshest possible environments for humanoid deployment — uneven surfaces, weather exposure, variable layouts. HD Hyundai faces a serious workforce shortage as previous generations of shipbuilders reach retirement age, while younger workers have not stepped in to join the industry in large numbers. If humanoids can weld in a shipyard, they can work almost anywhere industrial.

MARKET

HD Hyundai (329180.KS) is public on the Korea Exchange. Persona AI is private (Houston-based). Persona AI raised more than $10 million in pre-seed funding. HD Hyundai's parent also controls Boston Dynamics — notably, they're using external robotics startups for this initiative, suggesting Boston Dynamics' Atlas platform may not yet be commercially viable for industrial tasks. ---

Tencent Plugs OpenClaw AI Agent Into WeChat's 1 Billion Users

Tencent launched a tool on Sunday to integrate its WeChat messaging platform with the OpenClaw agent, deepening its push into AI agents that have become a key battleground among China's technology companies. The software, called ClawBot, will appear as a contact within WeChat, allowing users of China's most popular app with over 1 billion monthly active users to connect directly with OpenClaw, sending and receiving commands through the messaging interface. OpenClaw is an open-source AI agent that can perform tasks such as transferring files and sending emails on users' behalf. The pace of China's AI agent ecosystem is accelerating rapidly: Tencent earlier this month launched its own AI agent suite (QClaw, Lighthouse, WorkBuddy), Alibaba launched Wukong last week for enterprise multi-agent coordination, and Baidu quickly followed with OpenClaw-based agents spanning desktop, cloud, mobile, and smart-home devices.

Why it matters → The AI agent war in China is moving at a pace the West isn't fully tracking. Every major Chinese tech giant now has an agent platform, and WeChat's integration gives OpenClaw instant distribution to 1 billion+ users — a scale no Western agent product has achieved.
