Pentagon Locks Palantir's Maven Into the Budget — For Good

The Pentagon intends to designate Palantir's Maven Smart System as an official program of record, locking in multi-year funding for the AI-enabled targeting platform that is already deployed across every U.S. combatant command. Deputy Secretary of Defense Steve Feinberg signed the memo on March 9, stating that program-of-record status "will provide the stable funding and resourcing necessary" for Maven's continued development. An initial $480 million Maven contract was awarded in May 2024, with the ceiling raised to $1.3 billion in May 2025, alongside a $10 billion Army enterprise framework agreement signed in July 2025. Meanwhile, the FY2026 defense budget for the first time included a dedicated AI and autonomy budget line of $13.4 billion.

Anthropic Ships Computer Control for Claude — Your Desktop Is Now an AI Workspace

Anthropic announced that its Claude Code and Claude Cowork tools are being updated to accomplish tasks using your computer, becoming capable of opening files, using the browser and running dev tools. Users can now message Claude a task from a phone, and the AI agent will then complete that task. Claude computer use will initially be available to Claude Pro and Claude Max subscribers on macOS. Anthropic says its system will automatically scan activations to detect problematic activity, and Claude will always request permission before accessing new applications.

Google DeepMind Signs Agile Robots, Expanding Its Robotics Brain Empire

Munich-based Agile Robots entered into a strategic research partnership with Google DeepMind on Tuesday. The partnership involves Agile Robots implementing Google DeepMind's Gemini Robotics foundation models into its bots and the data collected being used to improve the underlying Gemini AI models. The effort combines DeepMind's foundation models with Agile Robots' installed base of more than 20,000 systems to generate real-world data, refine models and improve performance. The companies will work together to test, fine tune, and deploy robots across sectors including electronics manufacturing, automotive, data centers, and logistics.

Why it matters → This is Google DeepMind's second major robotics hardware partnership this year — earlier in 2026, Hyundai-owned Boston Dynamics announced it was partnering with Google DeepMind to use Gemini foundation models for its upcoming humanoid robot Atlas. DeepMind is positioning Gemini Robotics as the Android of robot brains: provide the foundation model, let hardware partners build the body, collect the data flywheel.

White House AI Framework Pushes Federal Preemption of State Laws

On March 20, 2026, the White House released a legislative blueprint for a national artificial intelligence policy framework. The Framework urges Congress to adopt a federally unified, innovation-oriented regime centered on preemption of state AI laws and a "light-touch" regulatory approach. White House OSTP director Michael Kratsios said the administration wants to codify the framework into law "this year." The proposal also requests streamlined permitting for data centers, regulatory "sandboxes," and no new regulatory body to oversee the technology.

Why it matters → AI industry leaders have strongly opposed state-level efforts, arguing that a "patchwork" of laws would hobble innovation and give global competitors like China a major advantage. If this framework becomes law, it would essentially neutralize California's and New York's more restrictive AI bills — a massive regulatory tailwind for frontier labs. However, it is unclear whether there is sufficient support in Congress, and implementation appears unlikely at this time.

MARKET

Broadly bullish for all AI names if enacted. META, GOOG, MSFT, NVDA all benefit from regulatory certainty and streamlined data center permitting. The largest cloud operators are planning to spend nearly $700 billion on capex in 2026 to meet soaring AI demand. Removing permitting friction could accelerate buildouts.
