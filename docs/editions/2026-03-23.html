Musk Unveils $25B "Terafab" — A Joint Tesla-SpaceX-xAI Chip Factory in Austin

Elon Musk announced the Terafab project, a joint venture between Tesla, SpaceX, and xAI, to build what he calls the "largest chip manufacturing facility ever." The project, estimated to cost at least $20 billion, will start with the Advanced Technology Fab in Austin, Texas, where Tesla is already headquartered. Musk said two types of chips will be produced: one for terrestrial purposes like Full Self-Driving and Optimus robots, and another more high-powered, durable chip to be used in space. Tesla says it is targeting 2-nanometer process technology — the most advanced node currently entering commercial production.

Why it matters → ** This is Musk's most aggressive vertical integration play yet — fusing three companies into a single silicon pipeline. If it works, it gives Tesla's Optimus robot and xAI's Grok a captive chip supply. But Musk has no background in semiconductor production and a history of over-promising on goals and timelines. Building a leading-edge fab from scratch is something even Intel struggles with.

Unitree Files $610M IPO — The First Pure-Play Humanoid Robot to List

Chinese startup Unitree Robotics filed an initial public offering application to the Shanghai Stock Exchange, seeking to raise 4.2 billion yuan ($610 million), in a test of investor interest in humanoid robots. The financials are striking: the company recorded revenue of 1.71 billion yuan last year, up 335% from a year earlier, while net profit on an adjusted basis rose nearly eight times to 600 million yuan. Unitree shipped over 5,500 units last year, occupying 32.4% of the global humanoid market.

Why it matters → ** This is the first real profitability test for the humanoid robotics thesis. Unitree is profitable — rare in this sector — and shipping at volume. The IPO target valuation of $7 billion represents a 4x jump from the $1.7 billion Series C+ round. If the IPO prices well, it validates the entire humanoid supply chain and puts pressure on Tesla, Figure AI, and others to show comparable economics.

Zuckerberg Is Building a Personal AI Agent to Help Him Be CEO

Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg is building a CEO agent to help him do his job, the Wall Street Journal reported on Sunday. The agent is currently helping Zuckerberg get information faster — for instance, by retrieving answers that he would typically have to go through layers of people to get. The project reflects a drive across the 78,000-person company to accelerate the pace of work, eliminate layers from its organizational structure, and change the day-to-day jobs of its employees.

Why it matters → ** This is a signal, not a product. When the CEO of a $1.5T company builds an AI agent to flatten his own decision-making layer, it telegraphs where agentic AI is heading in every corporate hierarchy. Meta is spending up to $135B on AI capex this year — Zuckerberg eating his own cooking makes the spending narrative more credible.
